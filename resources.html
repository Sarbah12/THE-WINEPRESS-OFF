Journal Prompts

Reflective questions to help you process what God is doing in and through you — organised by season and theme.

01
Season: The Pressing

"What is God asking me to let go of in this season, and what fear is making it hard to release?"

Sit with this for a few minutes before writing. Let the honest answer surface before the polished one.

02
Season: Waiting

"Where have I seen God's faithfulness in my past? How does that history speak to my current waiting?"

Write out at least three specific moments. The act of remembering builds faith for what is ahead.

03
Season: Doubt

"What is the honest question I have been afraid to bring to God? Write it out — He can handle it."

God is not fragile. Lament is a biblical practice. He meets us in the honest place, not the polished one.

04
Season: Healing

"What does wholeness look like for me? Not perfection — but what would healed feel like in this specific area?"

Allow yourself to imagine it. Hope is not naive — it is an act of faith when it is rooted in God's promises.

05
Season: Identity

"How has this season changed how I see myself? Is that shift from God's Word or from the wound?"

Our circumstances are not authoritative about who we are. Only Scripture holds that weight.

06
Season: Gratitude

"What has this pressing season produced in me that I could not have received any other way?"

Even if you cannot see it fully yet, ask God to show you. Thank Him in advance for what He is building.

07
Season: Purpose

"If my story — including this hard chapter — was used to help someone else, who would that person be and what would they need to hear?"

Our testimony is not just personal. It is often the lifeline for someone coming behind us.

08
Season: Obedience

"Is there something God has been asking me to do that I have been postponing? What is holding me back — fear, comfort, or unbelief?"

Delayed obedience is a form of disobedience. But grace is the place from which we begin again.
